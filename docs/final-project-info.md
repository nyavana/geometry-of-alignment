# Course Final Project Information

Source: `lecture/6699 Lecture1.pdf`, page 5 (`Math of Deep Learning: Course Logistics`)

## Extracted Information

- Grading is tentative: participation is `<= 15%`, the final project is `>= 60%`, and there may be a limited amount of homework worth `<= 25%`.
- The experimental part of the project can be implemented in Python or R.
- The final research project is done in groups of 4.
- The project can be mathematical, experimental, or a mix of both.
- Deliverables are presentation/slides, a paper, and code for numerics.
- The paper should include a survey on a topic related to the course.
- The paper should also include a research part that is mathematical and/or experimental.
- Experimental work should study properties of deep learning networks rather than focus on solving a particular problem by training on a particular dataset.
- The project may require writing code from scratch.
